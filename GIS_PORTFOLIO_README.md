A collection of GIS and remote sensing projects built using ArcGIS Pro, covering topics in planetary science, environmental analysis, and spatial data visualization. All projects use real-world datasets from NASA, USGS, and other public sources.

---

## 📁 Projects

### 🔴 [Mars Slope & Landing Site Analysis](./mars-slope-landing-site-analysis)
> Planetary GIS | ArcGIS Pro | MOLA DEM | NASA

Slope-based terrain suitability analysis for Mars using NASA's MOLA DEM. All major historical landing sites (Viking 1, Viking 2, Pathfinder, Opportunity, Spirit, Curiosity, InSight, Perseverance) are georeferenced and mapped alongside the suitability surface.

**Skills:** Raster processing, slope derivation, site suitability analysis, cartographic visualization

---

### 🌿 [NYC NDVI Vegetation Analysis](./nyc-ndvi-vegetation-analysis)
> Remote Sensing | ArcGIS Pro | Landsat 8 | USGS Earth Explorer

Temporal vegetation change analysis across New York City using Landsat 8 imagery over a decade. NDVI was calculated via band stacking and reclassified to produce change detection maps highlighting areas of vegetation growth and decline.

**Skills:** Remote sensing, band stacking, NDVI calculation, raster reclassification, temporal analysis

---

### 🌕 [Lunar South Pole Landing Zone Suitability — Artemis](./lunar-artemis-suitability) *(In Progress)*
> Planetary GIS | ArcGIS Pro | LOLA DEM | NASA LRO

Multi-criteria weighted overlay analysis identifying optimal landing zones for NASA's Artemis program near the lunar south pole. Suitability criteria include slope, proximity to permanently shadowed regions (PSRs), and water ice concentration derived from LRO mission data.

**Skills:** Weighted overlay, multi-criteria suitability analysis, polar stereographic projection, planetary GIS
